**Critical data rules — apply these EXACTLY:**

**Article paradigm:**

| Case | Masculine def/indef | Feminine def/indef | Neuter def/indef | Plural def/indef |
|------|--------------------|--------------------|------------------|------------------|
| Nom | der/ein | die/eine | das/ein | die/(bare) |
| Akk | den/einen | die/eine | das/ein | die/(bare) |
| Dat | dem/einem | der/einer | dem/einem | den/(bare) |
| Gen | des/eines | der/einer | des/eines | der/(bare) |

**Indefinite plural:** Use the bare plural noun without article (e.g., `"indefinite": "Hunde"`). There is no indefinite plural article in German.

**Noun modifications by case:**

- **Genitiv singular masculine/neuter:** Add -s or -es suffix to noun stem. Use -es for monosyllabic nouns and nouns ending in sibilants (-s, -ss, -ß, -z, -tz, -x). Use -s for most polysyllabic nouns. (e.g., des Hundes, des Computers, des Autos)
- **Genitiv singular feminine:** NO suffix on noun (der Frau, NOT der Fraus)
- **Dative plural:** Add -n suffix to plural noun UNLESS the plural already ends in -n OR the plural ends in -s. (e.g., den Hunden, den Kindern, BUT den Autos, den Frauen stays as den Frauen)
- **N-Deklination (11 nouns):** -(e)n ending in ALL non-nominative singular cases (Akk, Dat, Gen). NO -s genitiv suffix. (des Menschen, NOT des Menschens). Nachbar takes -n (not -en).
- **Plural-only (3 nouns: Eltern, Ferien, Leute):** singular is null for all 4 cases. Plural forms follow standard rules.
- **Uncountable/null-plural (~22 nouns):** plural is null for all 4 cases. Singular forms follow standard rules.
- **-s plural nouns (26 nouns):** Dative plural = nominative plural (den Autos, NOT den Autosn). No -n added.

**Feature field assignment:**
- nominativ: `"feature": "grammar_noun_declension"`
- genitiv: `"feature": "grammar_genitiv"`
- akkusativ: no feature field (omit)
- dativ: no feature field (omit — grammar_dative exists but not adding it in this phase to keep scope clean)

**Intro preservation:**
